case study

Subiektywnie o Finansach

It's currently one of the most popular personal finance websites in Poland, with a readership base of around 500,000 per year. It was founded as a blog in 2009 by Maciej Samcik, a renowned financial journalist, and in 2018 it transformed to be a financial news service

1. Overview

Main focus of the project was system development for organization and presentation of textual content in financial news service subiektywnieofinansach.pl. In other words - improving wayfinding and communication with the end-user of the website. The previous organization of content was insufficient to say the least, it was enigmatic and simply unhelpful. My team provided new solutions based on Information Architecture knowledge, competitor analysis and UX best practices. Two main issues were the categorization system and navigation, and the tagging system. We approached the task from a deduction-induction methodology point of view and were able to reduce the number of categories from 77 to 21 and the number of tags from 1503 to 45.

2. My role and responsibilities

I was a part of a two-person team under the supervision of a team leader. I conducted a competitor analysis which ended up with a presentation of the results to the management. My suggestion to decrease bounce rate of the home page was later a basis for further work. Based on gathered knowledge I took part in the process of reconstructing the whole two systems. I also suggested for example a color coding approach to new labels for navigation categories, which is still implemented today.

3. My partners and stakeholders

My working partner was a colleague of mine and a fellow college student at that time. The project was commissioned by financial news website Maciej Samcik Subiektywnie o Finansach.

4. Problem statement

Previous navigation and tagging systems were proven to be unhelpful for users in finding new content on the website. According to our report, the home page had a bounce rate of over 75% which was the highest from all the research websites. Reducing bounce rate was chosen to be the main indicator of a good change made in the system.

5. Users and audience

Users of Subiektywnie o Finansach are mostly people interested in or working in the financial industry. There are also people casually acquiring information about economy or personal finance tips. Data showed that the previous navigation and labels were unhelpful in finding a new content for both of these groups.

6. Scope and constraints

Initially the navigation consisted of 5 main categories and up to 77 categories total. All categories were predetermined and weren’t added new on the go, which is contrary to tags which were a sort of folksonomy. Each author could create new tags whenever a new article was added. This way of managing created a situation where there were 1503 tags total which was insufficient for content searching purposes.

7. Chosen design process

We wanted new systems to be fixed and more suitable for finding desired content. So we chose a deduction methodology for creating new categories. We used the existing categories to classify them to broader terms based on more accessible themes - like economy, tips for personal finances or investments. Additionally I suggested color coding the new categories to better differentiate them from a white background. More challenging task was to reduce the number of tags and for this we decided to go with deduction-induction methodology to basically create a controlled vocabulary. Based on the number of uses and common sense we differentiated main tags that would possibly stay and create new that appeared relevant. Next we created a process for cataloging every tag that didn’t fit our deduction approach. That way we were able to find new tags and categorize any other.

8. Outcome and results

The number of categories was reduced from 77 to 21 and the number of tags from 1503 to 45. Authors were instructed how to use new reorganized systems and were blocked from adding new tags. Tagging system was meant to be used as a part of a search bar feature to help find related articles. The new navigation bar showed a decrease in bounce rate index. Overall visibility of content was increased.

9. Reflections

It appears that deduction-induction methodology is a good way for creating controlled vocabulary and at the same time helping users move through available contents of the website.
